| Dimension | Advisor says | Perplexity says |

| #2 wedge | Indian fintech ops | Global sovereign-AI developer platform |

| View on global service | #3 (network mismatch) | #2 (matches YC global-first bias) |
| View on Indian fintech ops | #2 if no consultancy relationships | Not top 3 (same audit issues) |

### Why they disagree

**Perplexity ranks by potential EV** — total addressable market × defensibility × YC theme alignment. It's right about the long-run shape: regulator-side QA, if won, is more defensible than vendor-side compliance.

**Advisor ranks by 4-day-execution EV** — what can a 19yo solo founder credibly pitch to YC on May 8 and execute against in Months 0-12 with current assets and current network. Regulator-side QA is 12-24 month TTFR; that doesn't survive YC interview when partners ask "show me one customer."

The disagreement is mostly about **time horizon, not direction.**

### The resolution

Use **Indian BFSI as the YC entry, advisor's framing**, but **add Perplexity's regulator-QA as the Year 2-3 ambition** in the long-term vision section.

Concretely:
- **YC W26 application (next 4 days):** Indian BFSI compliance via consultancy-channel + per-customer adapter service. Pre-validated by working pipeline + measured results. *Conditional on user having a real consultancy contact name.*
- **Year 1 (post-funding):** close 1-2 BFSI pilots via consultancy or warm-network NBFC.
- **Year 2:** parallel pivot — pitch RBI/SEBI on the regulator-side QA platform (using bench data we'll have accumulated from Y1 customer training runs as proof). This is Perplexity's #1 — but we get there by earning it through commercial Y1.
- **Year 3:** global expansion (per-customer adapter service for US/EU regulated verticals once we have audit-track-record from Y1-Y2).

This sequence honors both verdicts: ship what's executable now, build toward what's most defensible long-term.
