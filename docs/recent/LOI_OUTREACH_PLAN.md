## What to ask for at the meeting

1. **Corpus scope** — how many internal compliance documents, what format, what regulator (RBI / SEBI / IRDAI / internal policy)
2. **Held-out test set** — can they nominate 20-50 question/answer pairs they care about, that we hold out from training
3. **Success metric** — substring match? token F1? human evaluation by their compliance team?
4. **Pilot terms if eval succeeds** — what would a ₹25-50L 90-day paid pilot look like in their procurement reality
5. **LOI** — non-binding letter of intent stating "We commit to a paid pilot if the held-out evaluation shows >X pp lift on our corpus"

The LOI is the artifact for YC. Even one with conditions is materially stronger than zero.

---

## What to NOT do

- Don't quote the IndiaFinBench number ("we score 30% on a public benchmark") in the cold email — it's the wrong frame for sales. Save the OOD-thesis for the YC partner conversation, not the customer pitch.
- Don't promise to cover IRDAI / PFRDA / FEMA out of the gate. RBI + SEBI are validated; pitching beyond that is fake.
- Don't send all 12 emails on day 1. Send 3 first, learn the response pattern, refine, send next 3.
